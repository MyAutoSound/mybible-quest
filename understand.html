Why this site exists.

MyBible.quest was built on a simple idea: the Bible is easier to understand — and more meaningful — when you can see the history, culture, and human experience behind it.

Our approach

Three lenses, one text

Every verse on this site is read through three angles, so context is never an afterthought.

01

Historical

Who wrote this, when, and what was happening around them? Scripture was written by real people in specific circumstances — exile, empire, occupation, community — and that background shapes what the words meant first.

02

Cultural

Ancient metaphors — shepherds, yokes, lamps, seasons — carried meaning that isn't always obvious today. We unpack the everyday images the original audience would have instantly understood.

03

Spiritual

Beyond history and culture, these texts have shaped how millions of people process grief, hope, doubt, and meaning. We treat that human dimension seriously, without insisting on a single interpretation.

What guides us

A few commitments

Educational, not persuasive

We explain context and history rather than argue for a particular theology or denomination.

Grounded in the text, not opinion

Every verse is drawn from the Bible's actual wording — rendered here as a plain-language paraphrase for clarity — and paired with its real historical and cultural context, not our take on what it should mean.

Open to everyone

Whether you grew up with this text, are curious about it, or are returning to it after time away, the explanations here don't assume prior belief.

Honest about complexity

Where scholars disagree — on authorship, dating, or interpretation — we say so, rather than presenting one view as settled fact.

Not a replacement for care

This is a reading and learning tool, not therapy, counseling, or pastoral guidance. Please seek qualified support for those needs.
